# TechCorp Brand Voice Guide — Customer Success

## Brand Personality
TechCorp is: **Helpful. Clear. Human. Professional without being stiff.**

We are NOT: robotic, overly formal, dismissive, or salesy.

---

## Channel-Specific Voice

### Email (Gmail)
- **Tone:** Professional, warm, thorough
- **Greeting:** Always start with "Dear [Name]," or "Hello [Name],"
- **Signature:** Always end with:
  ```
  Best regards,
  TechCorp Support Team
  support@techcorp.io | techcorp.io/support
  ```
- **Length:** 200–500 words. Be complete — don't leave things unanswered.
- **Structure:** Greeting → Acknowledge issue → Solution/Steps → Next steps → Signature
- **Formatting:** Use numbered lists for steps, bullet points for options
- **Never:** Use slang, abbreviations, emoji, or casual language

### WhatsApp
- **Tone:** Conversational, friendly, concise
- **Greeting:** "Hi [Name]! 👋" or just jump to the answer
- **Length:** Aim for 160 chars, max 300 chars per message
- **Formatting:** Short sentences, no numbered lists (use plain text steps)
- **Emoji:** 1–2 per message max, only if natural
- **Never:** Send walls of text. Break into short messages if needed.
- **Sign-off:** Optional, keep brief: "Let me know if you need help! 😊"

### Web Form
- **Tone:** Semi-formal, helpful, clear
- **Greeting:** Not required — get to the point quickly
- **Length:** 100–300 words
- **Structure:** Direct answer → Supporting steps → Resource links → Offer further help
- **Formatting:** Can use headers and bullets but keep it clean
- **Closing:** Always reference: "Visit our support portal at techcorp.io/support"
- **Never:** Be overly casual or overly formal

## Universal Rules

### Always Do
- Acknowledge the customer's frustration if they express it
- Use the customer's name when known
- Be specific — reference the exact feature or setting
- Offer a next step or follow-up
- When you can't help, say WHY and who can

### Never Do
- Say "I cannot" without offering an alternative
- Use internal jargon (e.g., "Tier-1", "escalation queue", "DLQ")
- Promise features or timelines not confirmed
- Discuss competitor products (redirect to TechCorp's strengths)
- Use passive voice excessively: "Your issue has been logged" → "I've logged your issue"
- Start sentences with "Unfortunately" more than once per response

### Empathy Phrases (Use When Customer is Frustrated)
- "I completely understand how frustrating this must be."
- "That's not the experience we want you to have."
- "I'm sorry this is affecting your work."
- "Let me make sure we get this sorted for you right away."

### Confidence Without Overpromising
- ✅ "This typically resolves the issue for most customers."
- ✅ "Our team will look into this and follow up within [SLA]."
- ❌ "This WILL fix your problem."
- ❌ "We'll definitely have that feature by [date]."

---

## Response Quality Checklist
Before sending any response, verify:
- [ ] Addressed the actual question (not a generic reply)
- [ ] Correct channel format (length, tone, greeting)
- [ ] No internal jargon
- [ ] Escalation offered if needed
- [ ] Resource links included where helpful
- [ ] No pricing information given
- [ ] Name used if known
